Help

Questions, answered straight.

The things people ask most. If something here is out of date with how the product actually works, that is a bug, tell us at contact.

Is Elusive really free?

Yes. No ads, no trackers, and we do not sell or share your data. We make no money from Elusive today. If we ever add a paid plan it will be for extra capacity or custom domains, never for privacy itself, and it will not take anything away from accounts that already exist.

What happens if I forget my password?

At signup you get a one-time recovery code. It is the way back in if you forget your password, so save it somewhere safe. You can generate a fresh one any time from settings while you are logged in. If you lose both your password and your recovery code, your mail cannot be recovered. That is by design in end-to-end mode: we do not hold anything that could unlock it for you.

What are the encryption modes?

Three, and you can switch any time from settings. End-to-end is the default: your key is made in your browser and we cannot read your stored mail, but a recovery code can get you back in. Keyfile is the strictest: you keep the only copy of your key and we store none of it, so nothing is recoverable, on purpose. Server-managed is the convenient one: we hold the key and can read your mail at rest, which keeps recovery simple.

Can you read my mail?

In end-to-end mode, which is the default, we cannot read your stored mail. One honest caveat: because Elusive receives standard email, an incoming message exists in plaintext on our server for the instant it takes to seal it to your key. Nothing is logged and it is never written to disk in that form. The full picture is on the security page.

How do aliases and personas work?

Alongside your main address you can make as many as you want. Some are permanent, some are disposable and disappear after a set time or the moment they are opened. Group any of them under a persona like "shopping" or "forums" to keep the parts of your life separate.

Can I use my own domain?

Not yet. Custom domains are on the roadmap and will be the natural home for any future paid plan.

Are there attachments, mail import, and mobile apps?

Attachments are live: you can send and receive files, and their names are encrypted at rest like everything else. Importing your existing mail and signed desktop and mobile apps are still being built. We would rather ship them properly than claim them early. When they land, this page changes.

Is Elusive open source or audited?

Open source, yes: the full source is public, so you can read exactly how Elusive works and hold us to every claim on this page. Audited, not yet: there has been no independent third-party audit. So you can verify the code yourself, but an outside review and a signed native client, which lets you check the exact code you run, are the next things we want to earn that trust properly.

Do you keep logs?

We do not log requests or the contents of your mail. What we do handle is delivery metadata, who a message is from and to at the moment it passes through, because routing email needs it. We never write that to a log either, and since update one the stored copy of a message keeps its sender and recipient sealed to your key.

Where do you stand on message scanning laws?

Against mandatory scanning of private messages, clearly. We wrote our position on the EU Chat Control proposal on its own page: On Chat Control.
